Code Nation equips students in under-resourced high schools with the skills, experiences, and connections that together create access to careers in technology.

Four out of five students who pursue tech careers decide to do so in high school, but young people growing up in low-income circumstances have

less access to computer science courses, internship opportunities, and role models that launch careers in tech.

With a volunteer teaching corps of hundreds of professional web and software developers, (including Joe!) Code Nation mobilizes the tech community to provide tuition-free coding courses and work-based learning programs to students who attend under-resourced schools.
